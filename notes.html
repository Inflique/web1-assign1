HTML Tags to Know:

HTML
Everything in a webpage is contained within the html tag, and I mean EVERYTHING. Without it, it would be like pouring water without a container.
Header
This is a "category" of sorts where you place the big title of the page, and surprisingly, STO likes to look at this tag a lot.
Article
If you wonder how websites arrange text and images into little building blocks, this tag is how. Normally, separate paragraph tags will still look grouped together on a webpage, but paragraph tags in different article tags will have that tiny gap between them, and I imagine you can align and arrange each article tag individually to spice things up a bit.
Nav
This is used to reserve a space for all the links that allow the user to switch between different pages. I think they're neat, and STO really likes this tag, too. Now that I'm thinking about it, how do they do those crazy dropdowns for online stores in their navigation bar??
Description List
Last but not least, the tag that this list is using! It's not an individual tag, but it is a group of three tags: DL, DT, and DD. DL is to identify this section as the list; DT is used for the title of each item in the list, and DD is the paragraph part that describes the item further. (This paragraph is within the DD tag lol)
